Improving job search with WhatsApp chatbots

Bringing personalised career guidance to where India already lives — designed for first-time job seekers navigating an overwhelming market through a familiar interface.

00 / overview

Job seeking in India is overwhelming — and lonely

India has over 180 million active job seekers. Platforms like Naukri and LinkedIn are the defaults — but they're built for people who already understand how to navigate career systems. For a first-generation graduate or a worker switching industries, they're deeply intimidating.

TalentAI started as a simple question: what if job seekers could get personalised career guidance in the same app they use to talk to their family? WhatsApp, with 500 million users in India, became our interface of choice — zero learning curve, zero download friction, and deeply trusted.

01 / the problem

The guidance gap for first-generation job seekers

Career counselling in India is expensive and concentrated in metros. Online platforms offer job listings, but not guidance. Most first-time seekers make decisions on advice from friends or family — often without salary benchmarks, skill-gap analysis, or resume feedback.

Existing chatbots were either too scripted (frustrating when you went "off-script") or too AI-heavy (users didn't trust the responses). We needed something in between: warm, conversational, and clearly purposeful.

How might we deliver personalised career guidance to first-generation Indian job seekers — in a medium they already trust, in a language that feels human?

02 / research & discovery

Talking to people who were actively struggling

We conducted 32 interviews with job seekers aged 21–35 across Tier 1 and Tier 2 cities — informal, over WhatsApp voice calls to stay true to the medium. We also shadowed 6 participants through their job search in a one-week diary study.

😰

Analysis paralysis is real

Participants browsed 40+ listings per session but applied to fewer than 3. Volume without filtering guidance led to decision fatigue and inaction.

📄

Resume anxiety is universal

"I don't know if my resume is good" came up in 27 of 32 interviews. Most had never received feedback and sent the same generic resume everywhere.

💬

Trust lives in WhatsApp

"If a real person sent me this on WhatsApp, I'd trust it." Trust for information on WhatsApp was far higher than on email or LinkedIn.

Key constraints: messages had to work on 2G. Rich media was optional, not essential. Every interaction needed a clear exit path. And the bot had to admit when it didn't know something — users trusted honesty over bluffing.

03 / design process

Designing conversation like writing a good script

Conversation design has no visual hierarchy, no whitespace, no hover states — the whole experience lives in word choice, pacing, and question order. We wrote 8 versions of each core flow before settling on the final ones.

01

Persona & tone of voice

A knowledgeable friend who's been through the search themselves — warm, direct, never condescending. Six tone dimensions: helpful, honest, human, focused, encouraging, clear.

02

Conversation flow mapping

Eleven core flows in FigJam: onboarding, job matching, resume review, skill-gap analysis, interview prep, salary benchmarking, tracking, and follow-up nudges.

03

Rapid prototyping via live bot

We tested by building — deploying early versions on the WhatsApp Business API and running live sessions with 10 participants per cycle. Feedback was immediate.

04

Failure-state design

A full week on "off-script" handling. Every error response was crafted to feel human ("I didn't quite get that — can you tell me more?") rather than robotic.

04 / final solution

Five flows that actually move people forward

TalentAI launched with five core conversation flows, each solving a specific moment of friction. The flows were modular — users could enter at any point and move fluidly between them.

01

Job matching

Personalised recommendations by role, location, and experience — with a swipe-style quick-apply via a templated message.

02

Resume review

Upload a PDF and get section-by-section feedback within 24 hours from the team plus AI pre-screening.

03

Skill-gap analysis

Compare current skills against a target role and get a curated learning path with free resources.

04

Salary benchmarking

Understand what you should earn by role, city, and experience — with negotiation talking points built in.

05

Interview prep

Role-specific mock questions as a daily 10-minute practice session, with nudges if you go quiet.

UX writing for each flow went through 4 rounds of A/B testing — comparing response rates, drop-off, and re-engagement after 48 hours. Final copy hit a 78% task-completion rate across core flows.

05 / outcomes & impact

Numbers that validated the approach

TalentAI launched as a closed beta with 500 users recruited through college placement cells in Pune, Nagpur, and Chennai. The first 8 weeks confirmed that conversation design — not app design — was the right medium for this problem.

78%
task-completion across core flows (vs. 34% industry average for chatbots)
4.2/5
average satisfaction across 500 beta users in exit surveys
3×
more applications submitted vs. a control group using Naukri alone

The project was featured as a CASUX case study and presented at National Design Week 2022. One participant landed a ₹12 LPA offer in Bangalore within 3 weeks of using the skill-gap + resume-review flow — our proudest outcome.

06 / learnings

What this project taught me

→

Conversation design is product strategy disguised as writing

The decisions that mattered weren't colours or layouts — they were what we ask first, what we never ask, and when to push vs. wait. It forced sharper thinking about user intent than any visual project.

→

The medium shapes the relationship

Users were kinder, more honest, and more forgiving over WhatsApp than they'd be with an app. Designing for an existing, trusted context is fundamentally different from designing a new interface.

→

Ship early, for real

Our most valuable iteration came from deploying a half-built bot and watching users navigate it live. No prototype would have surfaced the "off-script" patterns we found in week one.
