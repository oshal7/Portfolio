Designing connector experiences for cloud & on-prem data protection

How a shared connector journey brought consistency to three very different Cohesity integrations — GCP, MongoDB Ops Manager, and NAS — without flattening the domain expertise each one demands.

00 / overview

One connector model, three very different workloads

I led end-to-end experience design for GCP, MongoDB Ops Manager, and NAS workload integrations in Cohesity.

My work focused on the connector experience: helping Backup Admins connect a workload, discover protectable assets, configure policy-driven protection, and recover data with confidence. Rather than treating each integration as a separate feature, I designed a consistent experience model that could accommodate the different requirements of cloud, database, and file-storage workloads.

01 / my role

UX Designer, end to end

  • Led UX from problem framing through interaction design, validation, and engineering handoff.
  • Partnered with product management, engineering, QA, documentation, and domain specialists.
  • Defined consistent patterns across source registration, discovery, protection configuration, monitoring, and recovery.
  • Designed workload-specific guidance, prerequisites, validation states, and error-recovery paths.

02 / the challenge

One coherent workflow, radically different workloads underneath

Backup Admins protect a diverse estate across cloud and on-premises environments. Each workload has its own authentication model, topology, prerequisites, and recovery constraints — yet admins expect one coherent Cohesity workflow.

The design challenge was to make connector setup and day-two operations feel familiar without hiding the configuration detail needed for a reliable backup and recovery experience.

Q1How might we guide admins through workload-specific setup without overwhelming them?
Q2How might we make source discovery and protection selection predictable across integrations?
Q3How might we expose prerequisite failures early, in language an admin can act on?
Q4How might we preserve a consistent backup and recovery mental model while respecting each workload's capabilities?

03 / design approach

Three principles held the system together

01

A shared connector journey

I established a common journey across the three integrations. This shared structure reduced relearning for administrators moving between workload types, while giving each connector room for domain-specific controls.

1
Register the source
2
Validate connectivity & prerequisites
3
Discover assets
4
Select assets to protect
5
Apply a protection policy
6
Monitor backup status
7
Recover via guided workflow
02

Progressive disclosure

Connector setup can involve credentials, permissions, network access, agents, and workload-specific configuration. I used progressive disclosure to:

  • Start with the minimum information required to establish a connection.
  • Validate each step before asking for additional configuration.
  • Keep advanced options available without making the default path intimidating.
  • Provide contextual help at the point of need.
03

System state made visible and actionable

I designed status and error patterns around the questions Backup Admins need answered — is the source connected, which assets were discovered, which are eligible for protection, why is an asset unavailable, what action resolves it, and is backup or recovery progressing as expected. This shifted feedback from generic technical failures toward actionable operational guidance.

04 / workload chapters

Applying the model to three distinct connectors

Same journey, same design language — adapted to the authentication model, topology, and recovery constraints of each workload.

GCP

GCP connector experience

Context

GCP protection requires Backup Admins to establish a trusted connection between Cohesity and their cloud environment, then discover and protect the appropriate cloud resources.

UX contribution

I translated cloud-specific setup requirements into a predictable admin workflow, reducing ambiguity between "source connected," "assets discovered," and "assets ready to protect."

Design focus
  • A guided registration flow for cloud account and credential setup.
  • Clear validation feedback for connectivity, permissions, and discovery readiness.
  • A structured asset-selection experience that helps admins identify the right resources to protect.
  • Consistent policy configuration and recovery entry points aligned with other Cohesity workloads.
Outcome
Source-registration completion rate — pending Reduction in setup-related support requests — pending
Created a clearer path from cloud connection to protected resources.
MongoDB

MongoDB Ops Manager connector experience

Context

MongoDB Ops Manager environments are operationally complex. Admins need to register the Ops Manager source, discover MongoDB deployments, configure protection, and recover reliably while accounting for topology and node availability — with support for full and incremental backups, replica-set selection, and protection groups.

UX contribution

I turned a multi-system workflow — Cohesity, Ops Manager, and MongoDB hosts — into a coherent connector journey, helping admins understand what Cohesity was validating, which prerequisite was missing, and how to move forward without deep MongoDB expertise.

Design focus
  • A source-registration flow capturing the Ops Manager connection and required access details.
  • Discovery views that make the MongoDB deployment hierarchy understandable.
  • Protection configuration supporting workload-aware choices, including backup-node preferences.
  • Clear eligibility and error states for nodes that are unregistered, lack the connector, aren't snapshotable, or don't meet the selected backup preference.
  • Recovery guidance surfacing stricter readiness requirements, such as connector availability across target nodes.
Outcome
Time-to-register an Ops Manager source — pending Successful first-backup rate — pending Reduction in connector/prerequisite support cases — pending
Made a technically demanding backup and recovery workflow more understandable and actionable for Backup Admins.
NAS

NAS connector experience

Context

NAS protection introduces its own scale and configuration considerations: storage sources can contain large, hierarchical file systems, and administrators need confidence that the correct shares or volumes are protected and recoverable.

UX contribution

I applied the shared connector framework to a file-storage workflow while adapting the experience to how Backup Admins think about NAS resources: source, share or volume, protection scope, and recovery destination.

Design focus
  • A familiar registration and validation pattern aligned with the broader connector model.
  • Clear discovery and selection of NAS resources.
  • Protection configuration that helps admins understand the scope of what will be protected.
  • Recoverability-oriented status and recovery entry points.
  • Consistent patterns for prerequisites, warnings, and remediation.
Outcome
Time required to configure protection — pending Number of NAS assets successfully protected — pending Reduction in configuration errors — pending
Provided a scalable and consistent protection experience for on-premises file-storage workloads.
