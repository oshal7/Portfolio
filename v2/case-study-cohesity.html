00 / overview

A new way to protect MongoDB on Cohesity

Cohesity's data management platform protects enterprise workloads — but MongoDB, one of the most widely used NoSQL databases, lacked native protection through Ops Manager integration. DBAs had to manage backups outside the platform, leading to fragmented workflows and increased risk.

I owned the end-to-end UX for integrating MongoDB backup and recovery directly into the Cohesity DataProtect interface — giving administrators a single pane of glass for all their data protection needs.

01 / the problem

DBAs were context-switching between too many tools

Enterprise administrators managing MongoDB clusters on Cohesity had no native way to apply backup or recovery policies from within the interface. Every protection operation required jumping between Cohesity and MongoDB Ops Manager — a friction-heavy, error-prone process.

This gap wasn't just a UX annoyance — it was real business risk. Missed backups, slow recovery, and unclear protection status were direct consequences of the disconnected experience.

How might we let database administrators protect, monitor, and recover MongoDB workloads without ever leaving the Cohesity platform?

cohesity.com / data-protect / sources

02 / research & discovery

Listening to the people who live in the terminal

I ran 12 contextual interviews with senior DBAs and Cohesity platform admins across financial services and healthcare — the two verticals with the highest MongoDB adoption. Sessions included think-alouds of current backup workflows and a diary study of critical recovery incidents.

🔁

Fragmented workflow

Every DBA used at least 3 separate tools to complete a single backup cycle. "Cobbling together" was the most common phrase used to describe their process.

🕵️

Invisible protection status

No unified view of which clusters were protected, partially protected, or at risk — leading to a "hope it works" mindset around recovery.

⏱️

Slow recovery times

Average time to initiate a MongoDB recovery was 4.5 hours. DBAs manually pieced together backup metadata before even starting the restore.

Three user types emerged: Platform Admin (sets policies across workloads), Database Admin (day-to-day MongoDB operations), and Data Recovery Analyst (responds to incidents) — each with distinct mental models and recovery urgency.

03 / design process

From whiteboard to working prototype in 3 iterations

I ran a 3-day design sprint with engineering and product. Starting from journey maps for each user type, we identified the five highest-impact moments in the backup-and-recovery workflow where design could meaningfully reduce friction.

01

Discovery & source registration

How admins first connect a MongoDB cluster — Ops Manager credential flow and auto-discovery of replica sets.

02

Protection policy assignment

Mapping physical and logical backup options to an intuitive policy builder, without exposing technical complexity to non-expert users.

03

Monitoring & status dashboard

A live protection-health view across all sources — clear status indicators, last-run times, and SLA compliance signals.

04

Recovery workflow

Point-in-time recovery with a timeline scrubber — designed to get a DBA from alert to recovery-initiated in under 10 minutes.

Each iteration was tested with 4 participants on UserTesting, focused on task-completion rate and time-on-task for the recovery flow.

04 / final solution

One platform. Full MongoDB protection.

The final design integrated MongoDB backup and recovery as a first-class workload type in Cohesity DataProtect — consistent with existing SQL and VMware flows, but tailored to MongoDB's architecture (replica sets, sharded clusters, Ops Manager topology).

Key decisions: a topology-aware visualisation of replica sets so DBAs understood what was protected, a policy inheritance model letting platform admins set org-wide defaults, and a recovery confidence score surfacing backup age and integrity before an admin committed to a restore point.

05 / outcomes & impact

Measurable results, not just prettier screens

Post-launch usability testing and beta feedback from 6 enterprise customers surfaced strong validation across the three core workflows.

The feature unlocked a new enterprise segment — financial-services firms running large MongoDB deployments — that had previously cited the protection gap as a blocker to adopting Cohesity.

06 / learnings

What this project taught me

→

Enterprise ≠ complex UI — it means zero margin for ambiguity

DBAs are experts in their domain, not in our UI. Every label and status indicator had to be precise, because ambiguity in a recovery context has real business consequences.

→

Speak the domain language before designing anything

The most valuable week was reading MongoDB docs and sitting with DBAs explaining replica sets. It let me design with their mental model, not against it.

→

Recovery flows deserve as much love as creation flows

Most products optimise the happy path. For data protection, the "things went wrong" flow is where users are most stressed — so that's where I focused most iteration.
