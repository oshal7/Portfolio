00 / Overview

India's banks serve everyone — but their apps don't

Over 2.5 crore Indians live with some form of disability. Yet most Indian banking apps, including Axis Bank's, were not designed with accessibility as a core principle — leaving a massive and underserved customer segment unable to independently complete basic financial transactions.

This was my Master's thesis project: a thorough, end-to-end accessibility evaluation of Axis Bank's mobile banking app and internet banking portal, followed by a strategic remediation roadmap and an accessible component library built on WCAG 2.1 AA standards.

01 / The Problem

Accessibility as afterthought — not architecture

Axis Bank's digital platforms had grown rapidly, with features added iteratively over years. Accessibility was rarely factored in from the start — meaning screen reader support was inconsistent, colour contrast failed in dozens of UI components, and interactive elements frequently lacked proper labels or focus states.

For users relying on assistive technologies — NVDA, VoiceOver, or switch controls — core journeys like fund transfer, bill payment, and account management were partially or completely broken.

"How might we make Axis Bank's digital platforms fully usable by customers with visual, motor, and cognitive disabilities — without compromising the experience for anyone else?"

02 / Research & Audit

A systematic look at what was broken

I conducted a four-layer audit: automated scanning (WAVE + Lighthouse), manual WCAG 2.1 AA checklist evaluation, assistive technology testing with NVDA and iOS VoiceOver, and contextual interviews with 8 users who rely on screen readers daily for banking.

03 / Design Process

From audit to actionable design system

Rather than delivering a list of issues, I chose to produce remediated design artefacts — showing exactly what each fixed component should look like. This made the roadmap immediately actionable for engineering teams without requiring them to interpret abstract recommendations.

01
Colour System Overhaul

Created an accessible colour palette that preserved Axis Bank's brand identity while meeting WCAG AA contrast ratios — including a revised state palette for error, success, and warning contexts.

02
Component Library (WCAG-ready)

Rebuilt 28 core UI components in Figma with full accessibility annotations — ARIA roles, focus states, keyboard interactions, and error states — ready for developer handoff.

03
Remediated Flows

Redesigned the 5 highest-traffic user journeys (fund transfer, bill payment, account summary, profile, FD booking) with full accessibility built in from the ground up.

04
Strategic Implementation Roadmap

Produced a phased 18-month roadmap prioritising fixes by severity and business impact — aligned with Axis Bank's product release cycle and engineering capacity.

04 / Final Solution

An accessibility-first design system for Axis Bank

The final deliverable was a comprehensive accessibility design system — not just annotated mockups, but a fully documented component library, usage guidelines, and developer acceptance criteria. It was designed to plug directly into Axis Bank's existing Figma library with minimal disruption to ongoing product work.

✓

Every component included a "Before & After" annotation showing the original inaccessible state alongside the remediated version with contrast ratios, ARIA labels, and keyboard behaviour clearly documented.

05 / Outcomes & Impact

A roadmap that holds up to scrutiny

The thesis was evaluated by a panel including accessibility specialists and product leaders. The remediation roadmap was structured for real-world implementation — not academic exercise.

06 / Learnings

What this project taught me

→
Accessibility is a design problem, not a compliance checkbox

The most impactful fixes weren't technical — they were structural. Rethinking the information hierarchy, simplifying interaction models, and building in clear feedback loops benefited all users, not just those with disabilities.

→
Automated tools find the easy problems — not the real ones

WAVE and Lighthouse caught 40% of the issues I documented. The remaining 60% — focus traps, cognitive overload, misleading error states — only emerged through manual testing and conversations with actual screen reader users.

→
A roadmap is only useful if someone can act on it tomorrow

I deliberately scoped each phase of the roadmap to map to engineering sprint capacity. Abstract recommendations get shelved. Deliverables with clear acceptance criteria get shipped.
