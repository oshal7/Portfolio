00 / Overview

A new way to protect MongoDB on Cohesity

Cohesity's data management platform protects enterprise workloads — but MongoDB, one of the most widely used NoSQL databases, lacked native protection capabilities through Ops Manager integration. DBAs had to manage backups outside the platform, leading to fragmented workflows and increased risk.

I was brought in to own the end-to-end UX for integrating MongoDB backup and recovery directly into the Cohesity DataProtect interface — giving administrators a single pane of glass for all their data protection needs.

01 / The Problem

DBAs were context-switching between too many tools

Enterprise database administrators managing MongoDB clusters on Cohesity had no native way to apply backup or recovery policies from within the Cohesity interface. Every protection operation required jumping between Cohesity and MongoDB Ops Manager — a friction-heavy, error-prone process.

This gap wasn't just a UX annoyance — it represented real business risk. Missed backups, slow recovery, and unclear protection status were direct consequences of the disconnected experience.

"How might we let database administrators protect, monitor, and recover MongoDB workloads without ever leaving the Cohesity platform?"

02 / Research & Discovery

Listening to the people who live in the terminal

I conducted 12 contextual interviews with senior DBAs and Cohesity platform admins across financial services and healthcare sectors — the two verticals with the highest MongoDB adoption. Sessions included think-alouds of their current backup workflows and a diary study of critical recovery incidents.

🔁
Fragmented workflow
Every DBA used at least 3 separate tools to complete a single backup cycle. "Cobbling together" was the most common phrase used to describe their process.
🕵️
Invisible protection status
There was no unified view of which MongoDB clusters were protected, partially protected, or at risk — leading to a "hope it works" mindset around recovery.
⏱️
Slow recovery times
Average time to initiate a MongoDB recovery was 4.5 hours. DBAs had to manually piece together backup metadata before even starting the restore process.

Key user types identified: Platform Admin (sets policies across workloads), Database Admin (manages day-to-day MongoDB operations), and Data Recovery Analyst (responds to incidents). Each had distinct mental models and recovery urgency levels.

03 / Design Process

From whiteboard to working prototype in 3 iterations

I ran a 3-day design sprint with the engineering and product team. Starting with journey maps for each user type, we identified the five highest-impact moments in the backup-and-recovery workflow where design could meaningfully reduce friction.

01
Discovery & Source Registration

How administrators first connect a MongoDB cluster to Cohesity — including Ops Manager credential flow and auto-discovery of replica sets.

02
Protection Policy Assignment

Mapping both physical and logical backup options to an intuitive policy builder — without exposing underlying technical complexity to non-expert users.

03
Monitoring & Status Dashboard

A live protection health view across all MongoDB sources — with clear status indicators, last-run times, and SLA compliance signals.

04
Recovery Workflow

Point-in-time recovery with a timeline scrubber — designed to get a DBA from alert to recovery-initiated in under 10 minutes.

Each iteration was tested with 4 participants on UserTesting, with a focus on task completion rate and time-on-task for the recovery flow.

04 / Final Solution

One platform. Full MongoDB protection.

The final design integrated MongoDB backup and recovery as a first-class workload type in Cohesity DataProtect — consistent with existing SQL and VMware protection flows, but tailored to MongoDB's specific architecture (replica sets, sharded clusters, Ops Manager topology).

cohesity.com / protect / mongodb / recovery

Key design decisions included using a topology-aware visualisation of replica sets to help DBAs understand what was being protected, a policy inheritance model that let platform admins set organisation-wide defaults, and a recovery confidence score that surfaced backup age and integrity before an admin committed to a restore point.

05 / Outcomes & Impact

Measurable results, not just prettier screens

Post-launch usability testing and beta feedback from 6 enterprise customers surfaced strong validation across the three core workflows.

73%
reduction in time-to-protect for a new MongoDB cluster
<8 min
average time from incident alert to recovery-initiated (down from 4.5 hrs)
94%
task completion rate on core protection workflows in usability testing

The feature unlocked a new enterprise customer segment — financial services firms running large MongoDB deployments — that had previously cited the protection gap as a blocker to adopting Cohesity.

06 / Learnings

What this project taught me

→
Enterprise ≠ complex UI — it means zero margin for ambiguity

DBAs are experts in their domain, not in our UI. Every label, every status indicator, every error state needed to be precise because ambiguity in a data recovery context has real business consequences.

→
Speak the domain language before designing anything

The most valuable week of this project was reading MongoDB documentation and sitting with DBAs as they explained replica sets. It meant I could design with their mental model, not against it.

→
Recovery flows deserve as much love as creation flows

Most products optimise the happy path. But for data protection tools, the "things have gone wrong" flow is where users are most stressed and most in need of clear, calm design. That's where I focused most of my iteration.
