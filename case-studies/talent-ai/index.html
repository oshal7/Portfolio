00 / Overview

Job seeking in India is overwhelming — and lonely

India has over 180 million active job seekers. Platforms like Naukri and LinkedIn are the defaults — but they're built for people who already understand how to navigate career systems. For a first-generation graduate or a worker switching industries for the first time, they're deeply intimidating.

TalentAI started as a simple question: what if job seekers could get personalised career guidance in the same app they use to talk to their family? WhatsApp, with 500 million users in India, became our interface of choice — zero learning curve, zero download friction, and deeply trusted.

01 / The Problem

The guidance gap for first-generation job seekers

Career counselling in India is expensive and geographically concentrated in metros. Online platforms offer job listings, but not guidance. Most first-time job seekers make career decisions based on advice from friends or family — often without access to industry salary benchmarks, skill gap analysis, or resume feedback.

Existing chatbot solutions were either too scripted (frustrating when you went "off-script") or too AI-heavy (users didn't trust the responses). We needed something in between: warm, conversational, and clearly purposeful.

"How might we deliver personalised career guidance to first-generation Indian job seekers — in a medium they already trust, in a language that feels human?"

02 / Research & Discovery

Talking to people who were actively struggling

We conducted 32 interviews with job seekers aged 21–35 across Tier 1 and Tier 2 Indian cities. Sessions were informal, conducted over WhatsApp voice calls to stay true to the medium we were designing for. We also shadowed 6 participants through their current job search process in a one-week diary study.

😰
Analysis paralysis is real
On average, participants browsed 40+ job listings per session but applied to fewer than 3. The volume of options — without filtering guidance — led to decision fatigue and inaction.
📄
Resume anxiety is universal
"I don't know if my resume is good" was said in 27 of 32 interviews. Participants had never received professional feedback and were sending the same generic resume to every role.
💬
Trust lives in WhatsApp
"If a real person sent me this on WhatsApp, I'd trust it" — participants had high trust for information received on WhatsApp, significantly higher than on email or LinkedIn messages.

Key design constraints: Messages had to work on 2G connections. Rich media (images/documents) was optional, not essential. Every interaction needed a clear exit path. The bot had to acknowledge when it didn't know something — users trusted honesty over bluffing.

03 / Design Process

Designing conversation like writing a good script

Conversation design is different from screen design — there's no visual hierarchy, no whitespace, no hover states. The entire experience lives in word choice, pacing, and the order of questions. We wrote 8 versions of each core conversation flow before settling on the final ones.

01
Persona & Tone of Voice

TalentAI's persona: a knowledgeable friend who's been through the job search process themselves. Warm, direct, and never condescending. We defined 6 tone dimensions: Helpful, Honest, Human, Focused, Encouraging, Clear.

02
Conversation Flow Mapping

We mapped 11 core conversation flows in FigJam: onboarding, job matching, resume review, skill gap analysis, interview prep, salary benchmarking, application tracking, and follow-up nudges.

03
Rapid Prototyping via Live Bot

Unlike screen prototypes, we tested by building — deploying early versions on WhatsApp Business API and running live sessions with 10 participants per iteration cycle. Feedback was immediate and unambiguous.

04
Failure State Design

We spent a full week designing "off-script" handling — what happens when users say something unexpected. Every error response was crafted to feel human ("I didn't quite get that — can you tell me more?") rather than robotic.

04 / Final Solution

Five flows that actually move people forward

The final TalentAI product launched with five core conversation flows, each designed to solve a specific moment of friction in the job search process. The flows were modular — users could enter at any point and navigate fluidly between them.

Flow 01
Job Matching
Personalised job recommendations based on role, location, and experience — with a swipe-style quick-apply via a templated message.
Flow 02
Resume Review
Upload your resume as a PDF and get actionable, section-by-section feedback within 24 hours from the TalentAI team + AI pre-screening.
Flow 03
Skill Gap Analysis
Compare your current skills against the requirements of a target role and get a curated learning path with free resources.
Flow 04
Salary Benchmarking
Understand what you should be earning based on role, city, and years of experience — with negotiation talking points built in.
Flow 05
Interview Prep
Role-specific mock interview questions delivered as a daily 10-minute practice session, with follow-up nudges if you go quiet.
+ More
Application Tracker
A lightweight pipeline for tracking where you've applied, follow-up reminders, and status check-ins — all within the chat.

The UX writing for each flow went through 4 rounds of A/B testing — comparing response rates, drop-off points, and re-engagement after 48 hours of inactivity. The final copy achieved a 78% task completion rate across core flows in beta testing.

05 / Outcomes & Impact

Numbers that validated the approach

TalentAI launched as a closed beta with 500 users recruited through college placement cells in Pune, Nagpur, and Chennai. Results from the first 8 weeks confirmed that conversation design, not app design, was the right medium for this problem.

78%
task completion rate across core job search flows (vs. 34% industry average for chatbots)
4.2/5
average user satisfaction score across 500 beta users in exit surveys
3×
more job applications submitted by TalentAI users vs. control group using Naukri alone

The project was featured as a CASUX case study and presented at the National Design Week 2022. One participant landed a ₹12 LPA offer at a startup in Bangalore within 3 weeks of using TalentAI's skill gap + resume review flow — our proudest outcome.

06 / Learnings

What this project taught me

→
Conversation design is product strategy disguised as writing

The decisions that mattered most weren't colours or layouts — they were: what do we ask first? What do we never ask? When do we push, and when do we wait? Conversation design forced me to think about user intent more precisely than any visual project has.

→
The medium shapes the relationship

Users were kinder, more honest, and more forgiving when interacting over WhatsApp than they would be with an app. The familiar medium lowered defences. Designing for an existing, trusted context is fundamentally different from designing a new interface from scratch.

→
Ship early, for real

Our most valuable iteration came from deploying a half-built bot and watching users navigate it live. No prototype — no matter how polished — would have surfaced the "off-script" failure patterns we discovered in the first week of real use.
